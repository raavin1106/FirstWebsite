A Brief History About The Airport

Singapore Changi International Airport's(SIN) phase 1 construction was completed in 1981 which had cost $1.5 billion approximately. Phase 2 of the airport was completed in 1991 i.e Terminal 2 was inaugurated costing around $300 million.

The Terminal 1 was refurbished in 1999 with an expenditure of $400 million. Also, 2002 marked the arrival of the Skytrain which was a massive success, linking Central Singapore. In 2006, the Budget Terminal was inaugurated, to accomodate the increasing number of budget airlines.

Singapore Changi Airport place GST refund desks and currency exchange which enables the passengers to obtain Singapore Dollars (SGD) at a competitive rate of exchange. The off-airport check in service i.e web-checkin and telephone check-in to spend more time exploring the airport than waiting in the queue .

Latest Update: Singapore Changi Airport Terminal 4 was opened on 31 October 2017.
